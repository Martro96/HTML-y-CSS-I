Butlers & Home 24

While BUTLERS is an accessories shop primarily focused on the offline audience, boasting excellent store locations and affordable, stylish products, HOME24 is an online furniture shop, one of the leading platforms in the home e-commerce industry. It primarily operates online but also has 13 showrooms around Berlin where customers can physically inspect the products.


In 2022, Home 24 bought BUTLERS, and in June 2023 they called Ironhack with a brief showcasing a big challenge.


One of the main goals of this merger was to bring BUTLERS' offline users to the Home24 online shop and app, using BUTLERS' physical shops as a marketing tool to attract customers to the Home24 online shop, but somehow, this wasn't working.

Actual situation

Both websites still exist. Home24 also has introduced Butlers in its website.


They are introducing Home24 products at Butlers shops


Some locations share Butlers shop + Home24 showroom.


There is a Home 24 loyalty program that BUTLERS users can benefit from

Research

The project began with meetings with the Design department at HOME24, during which we discussed the main goals of this collaboration, aligned with their team, and reviewed the previous research that had been conducted.


Following this meeting, we had two main areas of work:


  1. Analyzing Competitors:This involved understanding where our brands were positioned in the market.
  2. Visiting BUTLERS' Shops and HOME24 Showrooms:This allowed us to gain firsthand insights into our current status and the steps we needed to take.

Competitors Analysis

  • We observe the popularity on online and offline market of both brands.
  • Home 24 is very popular competing with shops like amazon, wayfair, otto.
  • Regarding market positioning, BUTLERS and HOME24 operate in two very different sectors, with one focusing more on offline sales and the other on online sales.
  • Both brands enjoy high popularity, with HOME24 competing with strong platforms such as Amazon or OTTO.
  • The fact that HOME24 and BUTLERS cover different parts of the spectrum in the home decoration shopping market creates a significant opportunity, as together they address most of the home decor market needs.

User persona & User journey

First conclusions

  • We concluded that the main pain point is the lack of awareness the customer have about the connection between both brands.
  • Customers don't know that Butlers is now part of Home24, and they ignore the opportunities they have in both brands being members of the H24 loyalty club.
  • Originally, the design team was considering implementing screens, such as iPads, to encourage customers to download the app.
  • Despite being a potentially effective solution, the placement of these iPads in the stores as they are currently displayed was not eye-catching enough.

Our primary objective was to convert BUTLERS' visitors into HOME24 users.

How might we encourage BUTLERS visitors to download the Home24 App in order to enhance the flow of customers from offline to online?

We reached the conclusion that we needed to design a game in which the ultimate prize would be this 10% discount upon downloading the app.

Even though our first iterations took place on small devices, we stand out with the idea that these interfaces should be displayed on big screens that catch the attention of customers as soon as they enter BUTLERS. They should then be redistributed throughout the shop so that by the time the customer reaches the register, they have already downloaded the app and are ready to enjoy their discount.

The first iterations received very positive feedback. In our meeting with the UX team at Home 24, the solution was very well received. We were discussing the size of the screen, as they already have iPads distributed throughout the store and were considering implementing the game on these devices. However, we maintained our position of using big screens instead to achieve the highest conversion rates possible. We reached the conclusion that they will implement one big screen at the very entrance of the pilot store to see how it is received by customers.

We identified that the initial screen of the app does not motivate users to register. It immediately directs them to the Home24 home site. Therefore, we wireframed an alternative onboarding process for when you open the app for the very first time.

However, to make customers use the digital interfaces and guide them to download the app, we may also need to rethink the information architecture in the stores.

Next steps

  • Iterate and iterate, do more concept and usability testing.
  • Encourage customers to use the terminals. Improve IA to guide the customer to the goal.
  • Implement the two interfaces on interactive terminals in the stores.
  • Rethink the Information Architecture of the Butlers stores: organize and create branding methods and new graphic installations.
  • More research on customers moves. F.e. on how they use the QR codes.
