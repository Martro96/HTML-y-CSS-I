About Splitwise

Splitwise provides a convenient way to split various types of expenses, such as restaurant bills, rent, utilities, groceries, and travel costs. Users can create groups and add their friends or roommates to those groups, allowing them to track expenses collectively. Within a group, individuals can log their expenses, specifying the amount and the category. The app then calculates how much each person owes or is owed based on the shared expenses.


Splitwise simplifies the process of splitting bills and managing expenses among friends. It promotes transparency and fairness by ensuring that everyone's contributions are accounted for accurately. Whether you're sharing costs for a group vacation, managing household expenses, or simply splitting a dinner bill, Splitwise offers a practical and efficient way to track, settle, and manage shared expenses.

Redesign Challenges

  1. Restructure the home page with a clearer hierarchy, creating a more visual and direct access to the money you owe or are owed,.
  2. Make group creation easier and more straightforward, accessible from the menu bar.
  3. Remove the “add expenses” section from the homepage and keep it only within the open groups.
  4. Place the “debt settlement” section at the top of the group page and remove it from the scrollable sub-sections.
  5. Simplify the hierarchy of expenses with a comprehensible color code. Remove unnecessary information from the general expense overview.
  6. Reestructure the navigation bar, removing elements whose priority do not require them to have easy access, and add the posibility of creating groups/expenses directly in the nav bar.

Final screens

After working on the two main screens that needed major structure changes, the next steps were improving the work flow and applying small changes and features to the other screens. One of the major improvements here has been optimizing the space when creating a new group, adding all the information needed on the first screen, saving the user two clicks, and restructuring the architecture of the information to give more importance to settling debts.


This project has been particularly challenging for me because, despite having a somewhat clear idea of how I wanted the page to look from the beginning, I had to revisit my UI design choices many times to steer the application towards the minimalist and clear direction that I felt comfortable with.


Despite the back and forth, the result is quite close to what I had in mind initially. However, as the main learning point, I would emphasize the importance of dedicating more time to the mid-fi prototype in the first place and, secondly, creating a cohesive library of elements, a color palette, and a small style guide to work on sizes, typography, and colors before moving on to the high-fidelity prototype.
